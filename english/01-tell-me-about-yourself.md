# Tell Me About Yourself

Speaking practice for senior backend / full-stack engineers.

---

## 1. Core Speaking Chunks

### Responsibility Framing
1. "I was mainly responsible for..."
2. "My primary focus was..."
3. "I owned the entire..."
4. "I led the technical side of..."
5. "I was the go-to person for..."

### Impact Statements
6. "This reduced latency by roughly..."
7. "We managed to cut deployment time from X to Y"
8. "The system now handles 10x the previous load"
9. "This saved the team approximately X hours per week"
10. "Error rates dropped significantly after we..."

### Technical Explanation Starters
11. "The way it works is..."
12. "Under the hood, we..."
13. "The main idea behind this was..."
14. "Essentially, we built..."
15. "The architecture consists of..."

### Cause → Effect
16. "Because of tight deadlines, we decided to..."
17. "Since the legacy system couldn't scale, we..."
18. "Given the constraints, our approach was..."
19. "This led to a more maintainable codebase"
20. "As a result, the team could iterate faster"

### Trade-offs
21. "We had to balance X against Y"
22. "The trade-off was between speed and flexibility"
23. "We chose this approach knowing that..."
24. "It wasn't perfect, but it solved the immediate problem"
25. "In hindsight, I might have..."

### Reflection Phrases
26. "What I learned from this was..."
27. "Looking back, the key takeaway is..."
28. "This experience shaped how I approach..."
29. "One thing I'd do differently is..."
30. "That project reinforced my belief that..."

## 2. Grammar Patterns for This Topic

### Pattern 1: Present Perfect for Career Summary
**Structure:** have/has + past participle
**When to use:** Connecting past experience to present relevance

- "I've been working in backend development for about eight years."
- "I've built distributed systems across multiple industries."

### Pattern 2: Past Simple for Specific Achievements
**Structure:** subject + past verb
**When to use:** Describing completed projects or roles

- "At my previous company, I designed a data pipeline that processed 50 million events daily."
- "I led a team of four engineers on the migration project."

### Pattern 3: Past Continuous + Past Simple for Context
**Structure:** was/were + -ing, when + past simple
**When to use:** Setting the scene before describing an action or decision

- "The system was struggling with peak traffic, so we introduced caching."
- "We were running everything on a monolith when I joined."

### Pattern 4: Conditionals for Hypotheticals and Reflection
**Structure:** If + past perfect, would have + past participle
**When to use:** Discussing alternative approaches or lessons learned

- "If we had known about the scaling issues earlier, we would have chosen a different database."
- "If I were to do it again, I'd invest more in monitoring from day one."

## 3. Short Monologue (30–60 seconds)

> I'm a senior backend engineer with about eight years of experience building distributed systems. Most recently, I've been working on event-driven architectures and AI integrations — things like RAG pipelines and LLM-based features. I've spent a lot of time with Python, specifically Django and FastAPI, and I'm comfortable with cloud infrastructure on AWS and GCP. I also enjoy the leadership side — mentoring engineers, aligning technical decisions with business goals, and communicating across teams. I'm now looking for a role where I can combine deep technical work with broader impact.

## 4. Long Monologue (2–4 minutes)

> I'm a senior backend engineer, and I've spent the last eight years building systems that need to be reliable, scalable, and maintainable over time.
>
> My background is mainly in Python — Django, FastAPI, Flask — and I've worked extensively with distributed systems and event-driven architecture. I've also done a fair amount of infrastructure work: Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform, mostly on AWS and GCP.
>
> To give you a concrete example: at my last company, I joined when the platform was a monolith struggling to handle growth. My role was to lead the decomposition into microservices. The key challenge was doing this without disrupting the existing product. We couldn't just stop and rewrite everything.
>
> So we took an incremental approach. I designed the target architecture, identified service boundaries based on domain logic, and we started extracting components one by one. I was mainly responsible for the event bus — we used Kafka — and making sure services could communicate reliably without tight coupling.
>
> The result was significant. Deployment frequency went from once a week to multiple times a day. Incident response improved because failures were isolated. And the team could work more independently on different parts of the system.
>
> More recently, I've been focusing on AI integrations. I built a retrieval-augmented generation pipeline for an internal knowledge base. This involved vector databases, embeddings, and prompt engineering to make the responses accurate and useful.
>
> What I've learned over the years is that technical decisions always have business implications. I try to stay close to stakeholders, understand what problems we're actually solving, and communicate trade-offs clearly. I'm not interested in over-engineering — I care about building things that work and that other people can maintain.
>
> That's the kind of work I'm looking for: technically challenging, with real impact, and a team that values pragmatic engineering.

## 5. Technical Variation

> I'm a backend engineer specialising in distributed systems and Python-based services. Over the past eight years, I've designed and built event-driven architectures, primarily using Kafka and RabbitMQ for async messaging.
>
> At my last role, the main technical challenge was migrating a Django monolith to a microservices architecture. The system was hitting database bottlenecks under load — we were seeing P99 latencies spike during peak hours.
>
> I led the architectural redesign. We identified bounded contexts using domain-driven design principles and extracted services incrementally. For inter-service communication, I implemented an event sourcing pattern with Kafka as the backbone. Each service owned its own data store — mostly PostgreSQL, with Redis for caching hot paths.
>
> On the infrastructure side, I containerised everything with Docker and set up Kubernetes clusters on AWS EKS. We used Terraform for infrastructure-as-code, which made environment replication straightforward. Observability was handled through Prometheus, Grafana, and distributed tracing with Jaeger.
>
> The outcome: we reduced P99 latency by 60%, improved deployment frequency significantly, and the system could now scale horizontally without schema-level bottlenecks.
>
> More recently, I've been building RAG pipelines — using vector stores like Pinecone, OpenAI embeddings, and LangChain for orchestration. The challenge there is balancing retrieval accuracy with response latency, especially when dealing with large document corpora.
>
> I'm looking for a role where I can continue working on complex backend systems, ideally involving modern AI tooling and distributed data processing.

## 6. Business-Oriented Variation

> I'm a senior backend engineer with eight years of experience building systems that directly support business growth.
>
> At my previous company, I joined during a period of rapid scaling. The platform was struggling to keep up with user growth, and this was starting to affect customer satisfaction and retention. My role was to lead the technical effort to modernise the backend.
>
> The key outcome was enabling the business to scale without proportional increases in infrastructure cost. By redesigning the architecture, we reduced cloud spend by about 30% while handling significantly more traffic. Deployment cycles shortened from weekly to daily, which meant product teams could ship features faster and respond to market feedback more quickly.
>
> I also worked closely with stakeholders outside engineering. I translated technical constraints into business language — explaining why certain features would take longer, or why investing in infrastructure now would save cost later. This helped align priorities and avoid last-minute surprises.
>
> More recently, I led an AI integration project — building an internal tool that helped customer support resolve tickets faster. By surfacing relevant documentation automatically, we reduced average resolution time by roughly 25%. That translated directly into better customer experience and lower support costs.
>
> What I bring is the ability to connect technical work to business value. I care about outcomes, not just outputs. I want to understand what problem we're solving, who it affects, and how we measure success.
>
> I'm looking for a role where engineering decisions are tied to real business impact and where I can contribute both technically and strategically.

## 7. Improvisation Map

### Key Ideas to Cover
- Years of experience (approximately)
- Core technical stack (Python, distributed systems, cloud)
- Type of systems built (backend, event-driven, scalable)
- Recent focus area (AI integrations, microservices)
- Leadership / collaboration aspect
- What you're looking for next

### Optional Branches (expand if time permits)
- Specific project example (monolith migration, RAG pipeline)
- Metrics and impact (latency, deployment frequency, cost savings)
- Tools and technologies in detail
- Team size and dynamics
- Industry context (fintech, e-commerce, etc.)

### Fallback Phrases (if stuck)
- "To give you a concrete example..."
- "The main challenge there was..."
- "What made this interesting was..."
- "In practical terms, this meant..."
- "So the key takeaway is..."
- "I suppose what I'm trying to say is..."
