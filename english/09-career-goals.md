# Topic 9: Career Goals

## 1. Core Speaking Chunks (25 items)

### Direction & Vision
- "My long-term goal is to…"
- "Over the next few years, I'm aiming to…"
- "I see myself moving towards…"
- "The direction I want to take is…"
- "What I'm working towards is…"

### Current Focus
- "Right now, my priority is…"
- "At this stage of my career, I'm focused on…"
- "What I'm actively developing is…"
- "The area I'm investing in most is…"

### Motivation & Reasoning
- "The reason I'm pursuing this is…"
- "What drives this goal is…"
- "This aligns with my interest in…"
- "I've noticed that I'm most engaged when…"
- "What excites me about this direction is…"

### Progression & Growth
- "The natural next step for me is…"
- "Building on my experience in…, I want to…"
- "I want to deepen my expertise in…"
- "I'm looking to expand into…"
- "I want to take on more responsibility for…"

### Trade-offs & Realism
- "I'm aware this requires…"
- "The trade-off I'm willing to make is…"
- "This might mean…, but I see it as worthwhile"
- "I'm not looking for… overnight, but rather…"

### Reflection & Self-awareness
- "Looking back at my trajectory…"
- "What I've learned about myself is…"
- "I've realised that I perform best when…"

## 2. Grammar Patterns for This Topic

### Pattern 1: Future Continuous for Plans in Progress
**Structure:** will be + verb-ing
**When to use:** To describe ongoing future activities or gradual progression

- "In five years, I'll be leading a platform team and shaping technical direction."
- "By next year, I'll be taking on more architectural responsibilities."

### Pattern 2: Present Perfect + looking to/aiming to
**Structure:** I've [past experience] + and now I'm looking to [future goal]
**When to use:** To connect past achievements to future ambitions

- "I've built several distributed systems, and now I'm looking to move into a more strategic role."
- "I've gained solid experience in backend development, and I'm aiming to expand into system design leadership."

### Pattern 3: Conditional for Hypothetical Goals
**Structure:** If [condition], I would/I'd like to
**When to use:** To express goals dependent on circumstances

- "If the opportunity arises, I'd like to lead a greenfield project."
- "If I stay on the technical track, I'd want to become a principal engineer."

### Pattern 4: "See myself" + verb-ing
**Structure:** I see myself + verb-ing
**When to use:** To express vision without overcommitting

- "I see myself transitioning into a staff engineer role."
- "I see myself contributing more to architectural decisions."

## 3. Short Monologue (30–60 seconds)

> My main career goal at this point is to transition into a staff-level or principal engineer role. I've spent the past several years building distributed systems and leading small teams, and I feel ready to take on broader technical ownership.
>
> What I want is to influence architecture across multiple teams, not just within my own scope. I'm also interested in mentoring — helping mid-level engineers grow has been one of the most rewarding parts of my work.
>
> In the short term, I'm focused on deepening my expertise in system design and improving how I communicate technical decisions to non-technical stakeholders. I think that combination of depth and communication is what separates a senior engineer from a staff-level one.

---

## 4. Long Monologue (2–4 minutes)

> Let me share where I see my career heading and why.
>
> **Context:**
> I've been working as a senior backend engineer for the past five years, primarily in Python-based stacks — Django, FastAPI — and more recently with distributed systems and cloud infrastructure. I've had the chance to lead small teams, design systems from scratch, and integrate AI capabilities into production services.
>
> **Where I want to go:**
> My primary goal is to move into a staff engineer or technical lead role where I can have broader impact. I'm not necessarily looking to move into people management full-time — what interests me more is the technical leadership track. I want to be the person who shapes architecture, defines technical standards, and helps teams make better engineering decisions.
>
> **Why this direction:**
> The reason I'm pursuing this is that I've noticed I'm most effective when I'm solving problems that span multiple teams or systems. I enjoy zooming out, understanding how pieces fit together, and then helping others navigate complexity. That's where I add the most value.
>
> **What I'm doing now:**
> To get there, I'm actively working on a few things. First, I'm deepening my system design skills — not just building systems, but being able to articulate trade-offs clearly and make decisions that scale. Second, I'm investing in communication. I've realised that at the staff level, your ability to influence depends heavily on how well you can explain complex ideas to different audiences — engineers, product managers, executives.
>
> **Reflection:**
> Looking back, I think every role I've taken has moved me closer to this goal, even when it wasn't planned. Early in my career, I was purely focused on code quality. Then I started caring about system reliability. Now I care about organisational impact. It feels like a natural progression, and I'm excited to see where it leads.

## 5. Technical Variation

> My career goal is to become a principal engineer focused on distributed systems and platform architecture.
>
> Over the past few years, I've worked extensively with event-driven architectures — Kafka, RabbitMQ — and built services that handle significant scale. I've also gained hands-on experience with Kubernetes, Terraform, and cloud-native patterns on AWS and GCP.
>
> What I want next is to own the technical vision for an entire platform, not just individual services. That means defining how teams interact with shared infrastructure, establishing patterns for observability and resilience, and making decisions about technology adoption.
>
> I'm particularly interested in the intersection of AI and backend systems. I've worked on RAG pipelines and LLM integrations, and I see this becoming a core part of modern architectures. I want to be in a position to guide how organisations adopt these technologies without creating technical debt.
>
> To prepare, I'm studying how companies like Stripe and Shopify structure their platform teams. I'm also contributing to internal RFCs and pushing for better documentation practices — because at the staff level, writing is as important as coding.
>
> The trade-off I'm aware of is that this path means less hands-on coding. I'm comfortable with that, as long as I stay close enough to the code to make informed decisions.

---

## 6. Business-Oriented Variation

> My career goal is to move into a role where I can connect technical decisions to business outcomes more directly.
>
> In my current and past roles, I've seen how engineering choices affect everything from time-to-market to operational costs. For example, I led a migration to a microservices architecture that reduced deployment time by 60% — which meant the product team could ship features faster and respond to customer feedback more quickly.
>
> Going forward, I want to be in a position where I'm part of those strategic conversations earlier. I want to help leadership understand the trade-offs of technical investments — when it makes sense to take on technical debt for speed, and when it's worth slowing down to build something sustainable.
>
> I'm also interested in how engineering teams can be more efficient. I've seen organisations waste significant resources on poorly scoped projects or misaligned priorities. I believe a strong technical leader can prevent that by translating business goals into clear engineering roadmaps.
>
> To develop in this direction, I've been working on my stakeholder communication skills. I've started presenting technical updates to non-technical leadership, and I've been involved in capacity planning discussions. These experiences have shown me that the most impactful engineers are the ones who understand both the code and the business context.

## 7. Improvisation Map

### Key Ideas to Cover
- Current career stage (senior, looking to grow)
- Target role (staff/principal/tech lead)
- Technical track vs management preference
- Areas of focus (system design, AI, platform)
- Why this direction (natural fit, interest, impact)
- What you're doing to get there
- Awareness of trade-offs

### Optional Branches
- Specific technologies you want to master
- Mentoring and helping others grow
- Cross-functional collaboration goals
- Interest in specific industries or domains
- Open source or community involvement
- Building or leading a team from scratch

### Fallback Phrases If Stuck
- "Let me put it this way…"
- "The short version is…"
- "What it comes down to is…"
- "To be honest, I'm still figuring out the details, but the direction is clear…"
- "I think the most important thing for me is…"

## 8. Common Follow-up Questions

1. "Where do you see yourself in five years?"

2. "Are you more interested in the management track or staying technical?"

3. "What specific skills are you working on to reach that goal?"

4. "How does this role fit into your career plans?"

5. "What would your ideal next role look like?"

6. "How do you decide what opportunities to pursue?"

7. "What's holding you back from reaching that goal right now?"
