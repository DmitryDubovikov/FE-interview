üí¨ **Question**: Why is SMS latency often high?

---

‚≠êÔ∏è **Answer**:
- SMS messages can have high latency because of how the SMS system works.
- SMS is sent through a "store and forward" system, not real-time like instant messaging.
- Messages are first sent to the carrier's SMS center (SMSC), which then tries to deliver them to the recipient.
- If the recipient's phone is off or out of coverage, the SMSC holds the message and retries later, causing delays.
- Network congestion, routing between different carriers, and international delivery can also add extra delay.
- SMS was designed for reliability, not speed, so it is not optimized for low-latency delivery.
